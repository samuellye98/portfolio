About Me

Hi! I'm Samuel, an undergraduate double majoring in Economics and Information Science at Cornell University. I was born and raised in Singapore and spent 2 years in the military before college. I discovered my passion for technology during my time in Cornell and I have not regretted my decision since. I am passionate about building exceptional technological products that have meaningful impacts within the community.

This summer, I interned as a Software Engineer Intern at the Ministry of Home Affairs (Singapore), where I did full-stack development of a data visualization dashboard, as well as research and applied natural language processing technologies. During the summer of 2019, I was a Software Engineer Intern at Govtech Singapore, where I worked on an open-source platform for the Distributed Ledger Team.

On campus, I am a member of Lambda Phi Epsilon International Fraternity Inc., the Cornell Cup Robotics Team, and Cornell Venture Capital. I really love music and you can find me on Spotify whenever I can. In my free time, I enjoy working out, running, photography, and travelling around the world.
